Fisheries Management

  • Tech Stack: Python, Numpy, matplotlib

For this project, I investigated the effectiveness of different fishery management strategies for a fish population in the Kaipara harbour. I applied different Runge-Katta numerical solvers to solve the ordinary differential equations for each method.

A quota uses a fixed number of fish a business can catch legally. This turned out to be the least effective method, as the fish population is estimated to still drop significantly.

The kaitiakitanga method allows for a fixed quota, provided that it does not exceed a fraction of the population, otherwise it is reduced to that fixed fraction. This was found to be the most effective method for sustaining the fish population as it dropped initially, but quickly leveled off and became stable. The problem with this method is that it requires an accurate estimation of the fish population at all times, which is hard to calculate.

Finally, the periodic raahui (a tapu that restricts access) allows for fishing in the area for x number of years, followed by a complete shutdown of fishing for x years after. This also sustained the fish population well, and was easier than the kaitiakitanga method as it did not require advanced knowledge of the fish population.
